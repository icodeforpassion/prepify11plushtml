Exam prep

Ultimate 11+ exam study blueprint

Prepify11Plus Editorial • 9 min read

Families aiming for grammar school places need a structured 11+ exam preparation strategy that balances revision, routine, and motivation. This search-optimised blueprint delivers the must-have steps to keep your learner on track from the first practice paper to final exam day.

1. Map out a term-by-term study timetable

Create a twelve-week 11+ study timetable that rotates English comprehension, maths problem solving, verbal reasoning, and non-verbal reasoning. Use coloured blocks for high-focus practice days, lighter review sessions, and active rest. Stick the plan to the fridge and tick off wins together.

  • Print a blank weekly planner or adapt a spreadsheet to map core subjects and revision focus areas.
  • Start each week with a short diagnostic quiz to direct attention to sticky topics.
  • Schedule a weekly family check-in to celebrate progress and adjust the plan.

2. Layer proven maths mastery strategies

Mix daily fluency drills with rich problems so concepts move from recall to reasoning. Target key 11+ maths topics—fractions, ratio, algebra, and data handling—by pairing our interactive missions with quick-fire flashcards. Encourage learners to explain answers aloud to strengthen exam confidence.

3. Boost vocabulary and reading stamina

Strong comprehension underpins every successful eleven plus revision plan. Stack twenty-minute reading bursts with our vocabulary flashcards to lock in high-frequency grammar school words. Rotate fiction, non-fiction, and poetry to keep curiosity alive.

4. Train verbal and non-verbal reasoning skills

Dedicate mid-week sessions to verbal reasoning strategies such as code-breaking, cloze passages, and synonym hunts. For non-verbal puzzles, use tangrams or building bricks to recreate patterns before switching to digital drills. Timed mini-tests help learners practise staying calm while the clock is ticking.

5. Anchor wellbeing habits that prevent burnout

An elite grammar school entrance preparation routine includes rest. Add outdoor movement, mindful breathing, and creative downtime to every schedule. Try the “20-5” rule: twenty minutes of focused study followed by five minutes of stretching, sketching, or free play.

6. Rehearse the full exam experience

In the final weeks, run two complete 11+ mock exams under timed conditions. Replicate the desk layout, pen choices, and break schedule your learner will experience on test day. Debrief afterwards, highlighting accuracy wins before discussing errors.
