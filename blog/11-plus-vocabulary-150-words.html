Why vocabulary matters

Verbal reasoning, cloze passages, and comprehension questions hinge on precise word choice. A playful, consistent approach beats cramming ten new words the night before an exam.

Our demo uses spaced repetition logic to revisit tricky words while celebrating mastery streaks.

How to use the word bank

  1. Pick 5–7 words per session. Say them aloud, clap syllables, and explore synonyms/antonyms.
  2. Create a quick sentence or doodle that links the word to something memorable.
  3. Jump into the ElevenSpark vocab demo to practise in context.

150 spark words

The list below samples the words built into the demo’s flashcards. Meanings and synonyms appear in hover tooltips for screen reader support they are repeated inline.

  • abundant — plentiful (synonym: ample)
  • amiable — friendly (synonym: affable)
  • benevolent — kind (antonym: malevolent)
  • candid — honest (synonym: frank)
  • diligent — hardworking (antonym: idle)
  • emphatic — forceful (antonym: hesitant)
  • frugal — thrifty (antonym: extravagant)
  • gregarious — sociable (antonym: reclusive)
  • humble — modest (antonym: proud)
  • impeccable — perfect (antonym: flawed)
  • lucid — clear (antonym: confusing)
  • meticulous — precise (antonym: careless)
  • optimistic — hopeful (antonym: pessimistic)
  • prudent — sensible (antonym: reckless)
  • resilient — tough (antonym: fragile)
  • steadfast — loyal (antonym: fickle)
  • tenacious — persistent (antonym: weak)
  • vigilant — watchful (antonym: careless)
  • zealous — enthusiastic (antonym: indifferent)

Next steps

Rotate between reading, writing, and speaking activities so new words stick. Try a “word swap” challenge: replace a basic word (like nice) with three precise alternatives.
