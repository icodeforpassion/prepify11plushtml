11+ Maths: Fractions Made Easy

Why fractions feel tricky

Fractions combine proportional reasoning, times tables, and spatial awareness. Many learners memorise rules without understanding how parts relate to the whole. When denominators change, confidence disappears.

Before diving into procedure, make sure pupils can visualise fractions with fraction walls, cuisenaire rods, or simple paper strips. Encourage them to verbalise which piece is larger and why.

Teaching strategies that click

1. Connect to factors

When simplifying or finding common denominators, factor spotting is king. Ask learners to list multiples aloud: “Multiples of 6? Multiples of 9?” The overlap sparks the least common multiple.

2. Anchor to benchmarks

Use benchmark fractions such as 1/2, 1/3, and 3/4. Comparing 5/8 to 1/2 gives immediate insight — no lengthy calculations required.

3. Encourage reasoned estimation

Before solving, ask “What do you expect the answer to be?” This habit catches errors like adding denominators or forgetting to simplify.

Tip: Invite learners to sketch quick bar models in the margin. Visuals reinforce logic even in exam-style questions.
